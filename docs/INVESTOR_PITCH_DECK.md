# Fantasia Investor Pitch Deck

## Slide 1: Fantasia

**A local-first AI operations desktop for builders who need automation they can trust.**

Fantasia turns scattered AI tools, local scripts, creative pipelines, and guarded publishing steps into one auditable desktop control room.

## Slide 2: The Problem

AI tools are powerful but chaotic:

- Work is spread across chat, terminals, cloud dashboards, plugins, and local folders.
- Creative automation often makes unreviewed changes.
- Credentials, drafts, and receipts are easy to lose.
- Small builders need automation, but cannot afford unsafe live writes or platform lock-in.
- Investors and teams need proof of what happened, not just claims.

## Slide 3: The Product

Fantasia is a desktop conductor that coordinates local AI work:

- local service launcher
- operator session control
- FVE visual-engine workbench
- OpenClaw/Codex handoff lane
- COAI / WordPress publish-readiness panel
- receipt-first verification and rollback

It is designed for serious builders who want more autonomy without giving up authorship or control.

## Slide 4: Why Now

AI capability is rising faster than operational trust. The next wave is not only better models. It is safer orchestration: clear authority, local-first workflows, proof receipts, reversible changes, no hidden cloud dependency, and human-controlled publishing.

## Slide 5: The Beachhead

The first focused use case is website visual operations through FVE:

- review existing site images
- preserve images that already pass
- identify weak, broken, generic, repeated, or line-drawn assets
- generate or import better candidates
- map candidates to COAI slots
- block publishing until rollback and production guards pass

Current local receipt: `6` local publish candidates, `105` request-changes rows, `0` typed publish-ready rows.

## Slide 6: What Makes It Different

Fantasia is not another AI chat window. It is local-first, receipt-based, reversible, authority-scoped, desktop-native, built for creative production and operational safety, and designed around the owner's taste, brand memory, and approval history.

## Slide 8: Safety Architecture

Fantasia uses explicit boundaries: no direct WordPress publishing from Fantasia, no credentials in UI logs, no social posting without approval, no DNS/auth/plugin changes by default, no hidden production writes, no public dashboard exposure, and no secret leakage in release packages.

## Slide 9: Market Opportunity

Target customers include solo founders, agencies, creator-business operators, WordPress and local-site owners, design/dev teams using AI tooling, regulated or reputation-sensitive operators, and builders who need private local workflows.

## Slide 10: Business Model

Potential revenue lanes include desktop pro license, managed creative autopilot setup, premium connectors, team audit/receipt features, white-label agency workflows, support packages, and paid training for local-first AI operations.

## Slide 11: Moat
